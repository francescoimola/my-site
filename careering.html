Careering

Hey, it's Francesco. Here with what I can only describe as a public offer of collaboration - something along the lines of raising my hand in the school canteen and asking if anyone wants me to play in their band.

Now

I currently spend most of my time growing Never Not Ready. I went freelance in 2024, and it's been an exhausting but very rewarding journey so far. In my freelance work, I use design, words, and strategy to get people to buy stuff from other people (small businesses and freelancers). Or else book their time. At the very least, engage in their work.

While I’m more about off-grid marketing than doing your social media (I know some great folks who can help with that), I can make sure your foundations are strong, your community is up to date, and you're attracting leads even in your sleep.

I've recently launched a publication slash newsletter where I get to write about all the things that interest me and the NNR community—design, marketing, and sustainability-wise.

When I’m not working, you’ll likely find me walking around town, reading, spending time with people I love, practicing yoga (or some other form of socially accepted mind plus body activity), taking photos and making absurd little art on the web (and sometimes IRL).

Earlier

I previously enjoyed working at Blueberry, where I co-led communications, a brand refresh, and email marketing. Years ago, I helped the Galleries and Film & TV department at University of Greenwich with everything from events to copywriting, operations, design, and project management. It feels unreal that I’ve worked alongside so many brilliant creative people, from whom I've learned literally everything I know.

What I’m good at

I excel in generalist (or multidisciplinary) work with more or less identifiable creative components. I'm always happy to help with ops, web and project design, tool integration, inbound marketing, and UX writing. Thanks to my background, I'm pretty handy at helping small teams get tricky (read: time-consuming) projects done quickly and well. I'm also good at tapping into simple but effective storytelling, design, and comms to reach clients' goals. The best part? It's fulfilling work, and I rarely get bored.

Over the years, I've also had a great time working stints in internal comms! I enjoyed the freedom to experiment creatively and connect with communities both online and off. If the perfect internal comms role came along, I'd definitely consider it.

Where I see myself

In between all of this, I have some extra spare time now. And I'm open to collaborating with like-minded folks on both serious and silly work. In exchange for what, how long for, and how often will be up for negotiation depending on what the role entails.

Some things that are not up for negotiation:

  • Trust. In the process, that I do what I said I'll do, that I can ask for help when I need help, that I'm not in it to steal your job, that I'm being honest, that more often than not I can recognize my own mistakes, and that I can pivot if I need to.
  • Partnership. Collaboration should feel like a partnership that evolves. That means I need to get a sense of the bigger picture, where I can help, and which skills I need to contribute.

In short:

  • I'm open to part-time roles
  • These can be one-off projects or long-term positions
  • If this interests you or you know someone who might want to work together, I'd love to chat more. Drop me a message at francesco@nevernotready.club
